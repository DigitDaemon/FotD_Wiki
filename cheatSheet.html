Evocation:

Decide what you want to do: Attack, Shield, Gain Advantage, Counterspell

Attack:

  1. Decide how much power your spell needs: 2 shifts of power for each point of damage you want to do (you can split this damage between multiple targets); 2 additional shifts of power allows you to hit every creature in a zone with your spell. (SP)
  2. Subtract your Spellcasting and stunt bonuses from the shift power total, you take Mental stress equal to that number + 1 (SP - Spellcasting - Stunt Bonuses + 1)(Min 1)
  3. Roll your Will and subtract the roll from the power of the spell (SP - Will Roll = Overflow) [Enemies use the Will roll as the target to avoid the attack]
  4. If there is positive Overflow, you can choose to either take the Overflow as physical stress or risk collateral damage around you based on the strength of the Overflow

Shield:

A Shield is an Evocation-based full defense that costs one mental stress. Make a Spellcasting roll at +2, as usual for a full defense. If an attack manages to inflict enough shifts to overcome your armor, the shield breaks and you cannot block attacks with it anymore. This spell lasts until it is dropped or broken at no additional stress cost, but you cannot take any action except defense while itâ€™s active.

Gain Advantage:

  1. Decide what you want to do, the GM will give you a cost for that effect. (SP)
  2. Subtract your Spellcasting and Stunt bonuses from the shift power total, you take Mental stress equal to that number + 1 (SP - Spellcasting - Stunt Bonuses + 1)(Min 1)
  3. There may be additional things depending on what effect you cause.

Counterspell:

  1. Decide how much power your counterspell needs (Assessment roll). (SP)
  2. Subtract your Spellcasting and Stunt Bonuses from the shift power total, you take Mental stress equal to that number + 1 (SP - Spellcasting - Stunt Bonuses + 1)(Min 1)
  3. Roll your Will and subtract the roll from the power of the spell (SP - Will Roll = Overflow)
  4. If there is positive Overflow, you can choose to either take the Overflow as physical stress or risk collateral damage around you based on the strength of the Overflow
